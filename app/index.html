Mile, Mile & A Half

Web Design

I designed a website for the documentary Mile, Mile and a Half. I am a huge fan of the film, and thought it deserved a beautiful website to go with it. I began by creating a few moodboards. Moodboards help me and the client understand the overall look and feel of the website without getting bogged down with the details of the website. After a mood board direction was chosen, I created a few style tiles which provide a higher level of detail such as what some main elements may look like. After a style tile was approved, I created the site map to show the pages and the linked relationships among them. Next I created wireframes to map out individual pages of the site. Last, I skinned the wireframe, creating the finished website design.

Technologies / Tools : Adobe Photoshop

CSUN's Electronic Thesis and Dissertation Web Application

Web Design & Code

ETD is a web application Graduate Students, Faculty Members, Graduate Coordinators, and RGS Evaluators at California State University Northridge use for the process of theses completions. The old ETD website had a very outdated user interface that was not up to date with CSUN's branding.

I worked in close collaboration with front-end, back-end, project and product management as well as participating in weekly client demos and usability tests to complete this web application from start to finish. My job was to strip the old design from each workflow and apply CSUN’s UI kit, Metaphor, to ensure the web application maintained consistency with CSUN's branding and identity standards.

I was also in charge of solving the student's user interface and user experience problems. The students are the largest user base on the web application and there were constant phone calls and issues arising that could easily be fixed with enhanced user experience and interface design. The students were not clear on whether each phase was officially complete or not, who they were waiting on to approve different forms, and they were frustrated in only having the ability to see the next direct step to take in each phase, and not the entirety of all steps in order to plan ahead. It was also requested that members of a student's thesis be able to leave notes for them to see.

Technologies / Tools : Adobe XD, HTML5, CSS3, javaScript, jQuery, Gulp, Sass, Larval Blade, and CSUN’s UI kit and CSS framework - “Metaphor.”

Student Dashboard Before

The new progress tracking bar allows the students to easily see what phase they are currently in and how close they are to completion. The content underneath is separated into two sections: the steps to complete each phase with the visual que of a checkbox and icons that change based on each step's status, the status of the people needed to approve the work of each phase, and the new notes section. Upon usability testing, the master student's feedback confirmed a successful redesign.
